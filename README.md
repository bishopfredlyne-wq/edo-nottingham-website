Edo State Diaspora Nottingham is a community of people from Edo State, Nigeria, living in Nottingham, United Kingdom. It serves as a platform that brings Edo sons and daughters together to promote unity, cultural identity, and mutual support.

The group is built on the values of unity, culture, and empowerment, bringing together all Edo ethnic groups—Bini, Esan, Etsako, Owan, and Akoko-Edo—under one family. Through cultural events, traditional celebrations, and social gatherings, members preserve and showcase the rich heritage of Edo State while helping younger generations stay connected to their roots.

Edo State Diaspora Nottingham also focuses on empowering its members, especially women and youth, through programs, mentorship, and community initiatives. It creates opportunities for growth, leadership, and support, helping members thrive both socially and professionally in the UK.

In addition, the community contributes to society by supporting one another and engaging in activities that benefit both Nottingham and Edo State.

Overall, Edo State Diaspora Nottingham is more than an association—it is a home away from home, fostering unity, preserving culture, and building a strong, supportive community for present and future generations.
